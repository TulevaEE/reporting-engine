# February 2026 Report Review

Review against Kristel Raesaar's thesis principles ("Pensioniks kogumine kui heaolukäitumine", TU 2025).

## Where the report works well

- **Tone is authentic and human.** "Ootame põnevusega", "Sooviks midagi asjalikku lisada, aga kahjuks ei oska." Matches "professional but humanly vulnerable" recommendation.
- **"We" framing is strong.** "Tasahaaval teeme progressi", "meie jaoks" — supports social belonging.
- **Section 2 (new savers) nails social proof.** "Parim viis suurema hulga inimesteni jõuda on ikka oma kõige suuremate fännidest kogujate kaudu."
- **Section 4 applies nudge+support model directly.** "E-mail on hea tõuke andja, aga tugi tuleb ehitada ikka laiemalt."
- **Charts are clean and readable.** Y-axes from 0, consistent colors, current month annotated.

## Deficiencies to address

1. **Numbers without meaning.** KPI tables are raw numbers without context. "84,284 kogujat" or "1454 M EUR" — connect to what this means for real people.
2. **Report is inward-facing, not member-facing.** Mostly about Tuleva's operational metrics. Thesis frames everything around member wellbeing.
3. **Section 6 (unit price) uses fear without empowerment.** "Murettekitav, et II samba pikaajaline tootlus ikka inflatsioonile alla jääb" — thesis warns against frightening messages without clear action steps.
4. **"Pension" framing persists.** Thesis found "pension" creates distance. Commentary could frame outcomes in terms of freedom/independence.
5. **Section 5 commentary is dismissive.** "Raha on mõeldud ju kasutamiseks" — thesis says help people use money effectively, not shrug. Missed opportunity.
6. **No "next step" thinking.** Report describes what happened but rarely asks "what should we do with this insight?"
7. **Charts lack people-centric framing.** Titles like "Varade maht (AUM)" are financial/operational. Consider "Kui palju inimesed on kogunud" framing.
8. **Section 7 (financials) is purely institutional.** No connection to why Tuleva's financial health matters for members (e.g., enables lower fees).

## Summary

Voice and tone are a real strength. Main gap: report is metrics-first rather than meaning-first. Start each section with "what does this mean for our members' journey" and use numbers as evidence.
