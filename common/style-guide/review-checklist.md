## Three psychological needs (every section should support at least one)

### Autonomy
- [ ] Frame saving as freedom, independence, security — not obligation
- [ ] Reinforce that pillar assets BELONG to the person
- [ ] Link monetary amounts to life possibilities, not abstract totals
- [ ] Avoid language suggesting state tax or giving money away

### Competence
- [ ] Clear, simple messages — less is more
- [ ] Provide evidence-based "recipe" rather than information overload
- [ ] Include manageable next steps where relevant
- [ ] Don't use jargon that makes non-experts feel incompetent

### Social belonging
- [ ] Use "we" framing — collective journey, not institution talking at members
- [ ] Social proof: "X thousand people did this" > "you should do this"
- [ ] Members are the main characters, not the organization
- [ ] Celebrate collective achievements

## Nudge + support model (tõuge ja tugi)

- [ ] Nudge: simple, activating, context-sensitive, time-limited where possible
- [ ] Support: connect the step taken to broader values and meaning
- [ ] Never frighten without empowering — fear needs a clear, achievable action step
- [ ] Every section should answer: "what is the next step we can help people take?"

## Language and framing

### Avoid
- "Pension" where possible — feels distant, dusty, institutional
- "Säästmine" (saving) — perceived as restriction
- Abstract replacement rate calculations
- Fear-based messaging without action steps
- Top-down institutional tone
- Investment jargon for non-enthusiast audiences

### Prefer
- "Kogumine" (accumulating/building)
- Freedom, independence, security, peace of mind
- "Your money, your future"
- Caring for family — not being a burden, giving children a head start
- Concrete, achievable next steps
- Real human stories and examples

## Two user profiles

### Profile A: "Meelerahu hindavad tasakaaluotsijad" (peace-of-mind seekers)
- Don't need investment-technical discussions
- Need safe, clear guidelines — like health guidelines
- Frame saving as natural wellbeing hygiene
- Key message: "Saving is a natural part of a life that supports your wellbeing"

### Profile B: "Vabadust väärtustavad investeerimishuvilised" (freedom-valuing investors)
- Key message: pillars are NOT "investing for beginners" — they're the smart foundation
- Acknowledge sophistication, show pillars complement other investments
- "In Estonia, it's clever to first maximize pillar II and III — the tax advantage gives you an edge"

## Tuleva tone

- Professional but humanly vulnerable
- Mentor, not savior
- Tuleva's story IS the members' story
- Transparent by default — genuine, not performative
- "We" not "them" — maintain community co-creation

## Data presentation

- [ ] Connect numbers to meaning — what does this mean for real people?
- [ ] Present the essential clearly rather than everything comprehensively
- [ ] Frame growth in terms of people's wellbeing, not just AUM
- [ ] Use social proof in data: collective behavior numbers are motivating
- [ ] Journey-based thinking: "which people's next step creates the biggest impact?"
- [ ] Highlight critical transition points where intervention matters most

## The checklist question

For any piece of Tuleva communication, ask:

1. Does it support autonomy, competence, or social belonging?
2. Does it avoid the "pension" trap?
3. Is the timing right? (nudge vs support)
4. Does it respect the person as the expert on their own life?
5. Would this frighten or overwhelm, or does it empower?
6. Is there a clear but non-pressuring call to action?
7. Does it acknowledge the two user profiles where relevant?
